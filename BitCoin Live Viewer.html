Mathematical Parameters Explained

Simple Moving Average (SMA) Lookback
Functions as a low-pass filter to smooth short-term noise. It calculates the arithmetic mean over the chosen lookback window. Longer lookbacks create a heavily smoothed trendline with high lag, while shorter lookbacks mimic immediate changes quickly.
Relative Strength Index (RSI) Period
A bounded momentum oscillator mapping asset velocity between 0 and 100. Values exceeding 70 suggest computationally overbought environments (higher pullback risks), whereas measurements dropping below 30 denote oversold conditions.
Drift Factor (μ)
Represents the deterministic macro-direction vector within the Geometric Brownian Motion stochastic model. Positive values impose upward bias simulating a structural bull run, while negative drift forces a bearish structural decay.
Volatility Multiplier (σ)
Scales the variance parameter of the stochastic random-walk component (Wiener Process). Elevating this multiplier expands the mathematical dispersion cone over the 30-day forecast window, mapping heightened tail risk and chaos.
